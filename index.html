Audio samples from “Towards Natural Bilingual and Code-Switching Speech Synthesis Based on Mix of Monolingual Data and Cross-Lingual Voice Conversion”

Authors: Shengkui Zhao, Trung Hieu Nguyen, Hao Wang, Bin Ma

Abstract: State-of-the-art neural text-to-speech (TTS) synthesis models have dramatically improved intelligibility and naturalness of generated speech from text. However, one of the challenges to deploy the synthesis models is to synthesize bilingual and code-switching speech with speaker consistency. Although the synthesis models can be potentially trained on bilingual corpus, bilingual speakers are difficult to find and also expensive to make data collection. In this paper we explore the use of a mixed non-parallel monolingual corpus from a Mandarin speaker and an English speaker to build bilingual and code-switching speech synthesis models. We obtain the Mandarin speaker's English corpus and the English speaker's Mandarin corpus using a Tacotron2-based cross-lingual voice conversion (VC) method, where good naturalness and speaker similarity are achieved. Using each speaker's original corpus and the converted corpus, we then explore different neural TTS model architectures of Tacotron2, Transformer and FastSpeech for bilingual and code-switching speech synthesis. By using Transformer as a teacher model for Tacotron2 and FastSpeech, our experiments show that all the evaluated models can produce bilingual and code-switching speech with high speech quality. Subjective listening tests for both target speakers obtain mean opinion score (MOS) close to the professional records.
